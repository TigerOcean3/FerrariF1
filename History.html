Ferrari F1 History

Legends Never Die




The Story Begins

Scuderia Ferrari was founded by Enzo Ferrari in 1929 and made its debut in Formula One in 1950. Since then, Ferrari has become the most iconic and successful team in the sport's history, winning numerous Constructors’ and Drivers’ Championships. Known for its passion, speed, and legendary drivers, Ferrari embodies the spirit of Formula 1 racing.




"Racing is a great mania to which one must sacrifice everything, without reticence, without hesitation." – Enzo Ferrari




Ferrari Legends

Juan Manuel Fangio

The 5-time world champion raced for Ferrari in 1956 and secured the title with elegance and speed.

Niki Lauda

Twice a world champion with Ferrari, Lauda's return to racing after his crash is one of the sport's greatest comebacks.

Michael Schumacher

The most dominant era in Ferrari history came with Schumacher, winning five consecutive titles from 2000 to 2004.

Kimi Räikkönen

Kimi brought Ferrari its last Drivers' Championship in 2007 and remains beloved by fans for his no-nonsense style.
